Scotland's whisky regions

Highlands

Scotland's largest region, producing a vast array of styles — from light and floral in the east to rich and peaty on the northern coast. Home to iconic distilleries like Glenmorangie and Dalmore.

Speyside

Nestled in the Cairngorms, this is the most densely distilled region on earth. Renowned for elegant, fruity, and honeyed single malts — Glenfiddich, Macallan, and Balvenie call it home.

Islands

A loose collection of island distilleries from Skye to Orkney. Expect maritime salt and varying degrees of smoke — Highland Park and Talisker are the standard-bearers.

Islay

A small island with an outsized reputation. Islay whiskies are defined by powerful peat smoke and medicinal iodine character. Laphroaig, Ardbeg, and Bowmore are legends of the craft.

Lowlands

Gentle, approachable, and often triple-distilled. Lowland malts are floral and light — ideal for those new to Scotch. Auchentoshan is the most notable name in this style.

Campbeltown

Once the whisky capital of the world, now home to just three distilleries. Campbeltown drams are distinctive — briny, oily, and complex. Springbank leads the region's revival.

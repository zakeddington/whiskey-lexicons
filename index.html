Whiskey n.

/'hwiski, 'wiski/

Derived from the Classical Gaelic "uisce beatha," meaning "water of life." An alcoholic liquor distilled from a fermented mash of grain, such as barley, rye, or corn, and usually aged in oak containers.

Etymology

Old Irish • 12th Century

Variants

Whisky (Scotch/Japanese), Whiskey (Irish/American)
